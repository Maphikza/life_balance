Read Emergency Fund Primer

In this section, we will focus on the importance of being prepared for emergencies. It is essential to have some financial redundancy to handle unexpected events that may disrupt your budget and potentially lead to a debt spiral. This is not a precise plan, but rather a guide to help you think about the emergencies that are most relevant to your situation. For example, if your car breaks down and requires servicing, or if a water pipe bursts and needs fixing, you should have a buffer amount set aside to absorb the shock.

Read Life Insurance Primer

Although we may not want to confront our mortality, death is an inevitable part of life, and being prepared is essential. Life insurance is a way to provide for our loved ones who remain behind, especially if we are still in the process of building our wealth and savings. Without proper coverage, an unexpected death can leave our families in a difficult situation. We have all seen examples of families who have lost an income earner and struggled to make ends meet. It is crucial to think about how much coverage you need and to consider what you would want for your family in the event of your absence. This section may be difficult, but we believe it is an important issue to address in your life's journey.

Read Medical Insurance Primer

Although we strive to maintain good health, medical issues can arise despite our best efforts. These can place a significant financial burden on ourselves and our families. Since we cannot always prevent illness, it's important to prepare for the potential financial costs associated with healthcare. One of the most effective ways to do this is by purchasing medical insurance or medical aid. If you live in a country where healthcare is provided by the state, you may not need to worry about this section.

Read Disability Cover Primer

Dealing with a disability due to illness or injury can be emotionally and financially challenging. When faced with a situation like this, it's important to consider your ability to cope with the possibility of not earning an income while you recover or make adjustments to your career. You may need to have some income to pay your bills during this time. While this is not a comprehensive list of all the things you need to think about, it's a useful starting point to help you consider how much money you may need in such a case. This can give you some time to think about your next steps for living your life going forward.

You can listen or read the primer.

Read Income Protection Primer

Job security is never guaranteed, and being laid off can be a distressing experience. Unfortunately, it's a possibility we all need to consider. If we do lose our jobs, we must have basic protection for our income. We will still have bills to pay and will need time to find another job. While finding a new job can happen quickly, it may take several months. Considering income insurance is a good idea to protect against potential financial stress during this time. We encourage you to take some time to reflect on how you would manage if you were to lose your job. Depending on your personal circumstances and financial well-being, you may or may not require income insurance. It's crucial to have a plan in place to help you manage in case you find yourself going through something like this.

You can listen or read the primer.

Read Retirement Fund Primer

As we age, retirement becomes a reality we must all face. It's important to plan ahead for this stage of life, as we'll eventually reach a point where we're no longer able to work and earn an income. With people living longer and longer beyond retirement age, it's crucial to ensure that you have enough funds to support yourself throughout your retirement years while also considering the possibility of leaving an inheritance. Your approach to retirement planning will depend on your personal philosophy and financial goals. This section serves as a guide to help you consider this aspect of life and ensure that you're prepared for it.
